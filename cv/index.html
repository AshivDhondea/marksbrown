University Education

  • PhD — Final Year — Photonic Systems Development, University College London, 09/2011 to Present
    • An Interdisciplinary PhD combining studies of scintillator detectors with photonic reflection metasurfaces.
    • Topics covered include : the statistical analysis of the timing performance of scintillator detectors used in Positron Emission Tomography, experimental measurements of the radiation and optical properties of scintillator detectors and design of a nanoscaled periodic structure for preferentially scattering light.
    • This PhD is completed in collaboration with the Crystal Clear Collaboration based at CERN.
    • A more complete description of the research being conducted can be found at www.markbrown.io/research.
  • MRes — Pass — Photonic Systems Development, University of Cambridge, 09/2010 to 09/2011
    • A ‘Master of Research’ one year postgraduate masters comprised of two research projects and five taught modules. A description of the centre’s work can be found here.
    • 1st Research Project : Spherical lens distortion correction for holographic projectors.
    • 2nd Research Project : One Dimension Photonic Crystals for increased extraction of light from Scintillator Crystals.
  • MPhys — 2:1 (Hons) — Physics, University of Manchester, 09/2006 to 06/2010
    • Twelve modules per year covering taught and experimental topics over 4 years.
    • A wide variety of physics studied (and enjoyed) including electrodynamics, solid state physics and nanotechnology.
    • Two taught modules on programming in C++. Self taught from this period onwards in LaTeX.
    • First 4th year project : alpha-gamma correlation studies.
    • Second 4th year project : A study of liquid crystal infused photonic crystal fibres as a potential electric field sensor.
