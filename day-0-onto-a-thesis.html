Day 0 : Onto a thesis

Date Thu 10 July 2014

The summer remaining is a mere 80 days long. And frankly no amount of coffee (or whisky) is going to make it any longer. So today we’re on day zero…crap.

thesis : https://github.com/marksbrown/phd_thesis/tree/2d5cb0319fc4c03ec1fa86d370862c79cff45a1f

Okay so my thesis as of 10 or so pm, (with Laphroaig in hand) consists of an outline and a slightly more detailed outline!

Over the next few months I will be writing a post every day on my [death march|progress] towards a thesis. Perhaps on a more upbeat point, I hope to point out anything useful I’ve picked up along the way of my 8 years of academia. At the very least it’ll be a (terrifying) insight into caffeine poisoning. So….

Today I …

  1. The sub-outline for the first two chapters has been written.
  2. New and interesting measurements collected from the TCSPC (Time Correlated Single Photon Counting).
  3. UV spectrophotometer measurements show PTFE tape has a transmittance above zero.

…clear right? Right?? Of course not. What the heck is PTFE or TCSPC? What’s with two acronyms?! So right, the above isn’t the interesting bits. What they mean is! So…

  • In my mind a detailed plan means I can write those chapters quickly since I can picture their layout.
  • These measurements show how my samples respond with light at very short time scales. Best of all the measurements show some interesting behaviour! Scientists (amongst others) love interesting.
  • PTFE tape (commonly bought from BnQ to seal leaky pipes) is used loads in my work. It seems to have light leak through even several layers of the stuff. This will need confirming.

End
